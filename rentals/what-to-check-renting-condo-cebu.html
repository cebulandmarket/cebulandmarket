What to Check Before Renting a Condo in Cebu

A complete checklist so you don't miss anything before signing a condo lease in Cebu.

Condos are one of the most popular rental options in Cebu, especially in areas like IT Park, Cebu Business Park, Banilad, Mandaue, and Lapu-Lapu. They offer convenience, security, and modern amenities. But before you sign a lease, there are important things to check. Here's your complete condo rental checklist.

1. Check the Unit Condition

When you visit the condo unit, inspect it thoroughly:

  • Walls and ceiling: Look for cracks, water stains, peeling paint, or mold
  • Floors: Check for loose tiles, scratches, or uneven surfaces
  • Windows and doors: Make sure they open and close properly, and locks work
  • Bathroom: Flush the toilet, run the shower, check for leaks under the sink
  • Kitchen: Test the stove, faucet, and check cabinet condition
  • Electrical: Test all light switches and outlets. Bring a phone charger to test
  • Air conditioning: Turn it on and check if it cools properly and doesn't smell

2. Verify What's Included

Condos in Cebu can be rented as bare, semi-furnished, or fully furnished. Clarify exactly what comes with the unit:

  • Bare: Empty unit — you provide everything (bed, appliances, furniture)
  • Semi-furnished: Usually includes AC, basic kitchen fixtures, sometimes a bed frame
  • Fully furnished: Includes bed, sofa, appliances, kitchenware, and sometimes Wi-Fi

Get a written inventory list of all items included. This protects both you and the landlord.

3. Understand All the Fees

Condo rentals often come with additional fees beyond just the monthly rent:

  • Association dues: Most condos charge monthly association dues (₱1,500 – ₱5,000+). Ask if this is included in the rent or paid separately.
  • Parking fee: If you have a car, ask about parking availability and cost (₱2,000 – ₱5,000/month)
  • Electricity and water: Usually separate. Condo electricity can be higher than regular residential rates in some buildings.
  • Internet: Check if Wi-Fi is included or if you need to set up your own
  • Move-in/move-out fees: Some condo buildings charge a one-time fee for moving furniture in or out

4. Check Building Amenities and Rules

Different condo buildings offer different amenities. Check what's available:

  • Pool, gym, function rooms: Are they maintained? Are there usage fees?
  • Security: 24/7 guards, CCTV, key card access?
  • Elevators: How many? Do they work reliably?
  • Laundry: Is there a laundry area in the building, or do you need to go outside?
  • Visitor policy: Some condos have strict rules about overnight guests
  • Pet policy: Many condos in Cebu do not allow pets. Confirm before moving in.
  • Noise rules: Ask about quiet hours, especially if you work night shifts

5. Check the Location and Surroundings

The building itself matters, but so does everything around it:

  • Commute: How far is it from your workplace or school? Is there traffic?
  • Transportation: Are there jeepneys, taxis, or Grab nearby?
  • Essentials: Grocery stores, pharmacies, restaurants, ATMs within walking distance?
  • Safety: Is the area well-lit at night? Ask current residents about the neighborhood
  • Flooding: Some areas in Cebu flood during heavy rain. Ask building admin or neighbors

6. Review the Lease Terms

Condo leases often have stricter terms than house rentals. Pay attention to:

  • Minimum lease period: Usually 6 months to 1 year. Breaking early may mean losing your deposit.
  • Rent increase: When can the landlord raise the rent, and by how much?
  • Deposit return: How long after move-out will you get your deposit back? What deductions are allowed?
  • Subletting: Can you sublet the unit if you need to leave early?
  • Renewal: What happens at the end of the lease? Auto-renewal or renegotiation?

7. Verify the Landlord

Make sure you're dealing with the actual unit owner or an authorized representative:

  • Ask for a copy of the Condominium Certificate of Title (CCT) or authorization letter
  • Check the landlord's government ID
  • If dealing through a platform like CebuRentMarket, the landlord has already been verified

8. Red Flags to Watch For

  • Landlord refuses to show the actual unit (only shows "sample" units)
  • No written lease agreement — just verbal promises
  • Pressure to pay immediately without time to review
  • Rent that seems unusually low for the location and unit type
  • Landlord can't provide proof of ownership
  • Building has visible maintenance issues (broken elevators, dirty hallways, no security)
